The Scheme

Solar pumps by day. Income all year.

Launched in 2019, PM KUSUM Yojana is a Government of India scheme to promote solar energy in agriculture — helping farmers deploy solar pumps and power plants, reduce electricity and diesel costs, and earn from surplus power sold to the grid.

Component C · Grid-connected solarisation

Solarising the pumpsand the feeders.

C1 — Individual Pump Solarisation

One pump, one plant

Individual grid-connected agricultural pumps are converted to run on solar. The pump draws free solar power through the day — and any excess energy is supplied to the grid, turning irrigation into income.

Grid-connected pumpsSurplus to gridLower running costs
C2 — Feeder Level Solarisation

Whole feeders, solarised

Entire agricultural feeders are solarised at once — guaranteeing dependable daytime power for many pumps together while reducing DISCOM losses. In Maharashtra, the Solar Agri-Feeder Scheme has benefited around 8 lakh farmers.

Full agricultural feedersReliable daytime powerLower DISCOM losses
